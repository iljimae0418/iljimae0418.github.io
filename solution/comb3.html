We start from (0,0) and after 10 steps, we will reach some (x,y), where $$ |x| + |y| \leq 10 $$ Furthermore, with each step, the parity of either the x or the y coordinate will change. This means that after 10 steps, we can only be in (x,y) such that \(x+y\) is even. Since \(x\) can be any integer from -10 to 10, and for each choice of \(x\), we have $$ 11-|x| $$ possibilities for the value of y. This means that there are totally $$ 2 \times (1+2+...+10) + 11 = \boxed{121} $$ possibilites.
