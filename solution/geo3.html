This problem is all about similar triangles. First, let \(AF = FG = GB = x, CB = y\), then \(3xy = 70\). Note that \(\triangle ECH \sim \triangle HAF\). The length ratio for these two is 3:2. Thus, $$ [\triangle ECH] = \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{3x}{2} \times \frac{3y}{5} = \frac{21}{2}$$ \(\triangle ECJ \sim \triangle JAG\), and the length ratio for these two is 3:4. Using a similar logic, $$ [\triangle ECJ] = \frac{3x}{4} \times \frac{3y}{7} = \frac{15}{2} $$ and our answer is \(\frac{21}{2} - \frac{15}{2} = 3\).
