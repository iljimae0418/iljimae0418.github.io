Before we solve this problem, let's make one observation about powers of 2. The powers of 2 have units digit that goes in cycle of 4, i.e. \(2,4,8,6\). Using this fact, $$ k \equiv 2008^2 + 2^{2008} \equiv 8^2 + 6 \equiv = 4+6 \equiv 0 \mod 10 $$ Thus \(k^2 \equiv 0 \mod 10\) and \(k = 2008^2 + 2^{2008} \) is a multiple of 4, so $$ 2^k \equiv 2^4 \equiv 6 \mod 10 $$. $$ \therefore k^2 + 2^k \equiv 6 \mod 10 $$ so the units digit is 6.
