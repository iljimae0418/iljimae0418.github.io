Note that our goal is to make the x,y,z coordinates of the midpoint of the two chosen points to be integer values. Suppose the two points we chose are \(p_1,p2\).
  • All x,y,z coordinates of \(p_1,p_2\) are even. There are \(2 \times 2 \times 3 = 12\) such points in S, and there are \(\frac{12 \times 11}{2} = 66\) ways to choose such points.
  • All x,y,z coordinates of \(p_1,p_2\) are odd. There are \(1 \times 2 \times 2 = 4\) such points in S, and there are \(\frac{4 \times 3}{2} = 6\) ways to choose such points.
  • Only x coordinates of \(p_1,p_2\) are odd. Using a similar logic as above,there are 6 such points and 15 ways to 2 out of these
  • Only y coordinates of \(p_1,p_2\) are odd. There are 12 such points and 66 ways to choose 2 out of these

  • Only y coordinates of \(p_1,p_2\) are even. There are 4 such points and 6 ways to choose 2 out of these
